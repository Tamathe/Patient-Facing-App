# Patient Centered — Feature Specs

Feature specs for the patient-facing app: seven upstream/access PRDs (01–07), plus implementation and demo-first specs, each grounded in the existing coach + safety-gate, medication-adherence loop, and Food Lens primitives.

| # | Spec | Sharpest risks |
|---|------|----------------|
| 01 | [Silent-Killer Early Detection & Risk Stratification](01-early-detection.md) | FDA SaMD/CDS boundary is unresolved and blocking for P1: computing and displaying ASCVD/FRAX/ADA risk scores and screening recommendations sits at the Clinical Decision Support line, and the whole design (transparent inputs, cited sources, clinician-in-the-loop, no autonomous action) is a bet on staying non-device that needs formal regulatory sign-off before broad exposure.; The shipped PatientProfile has no age, sex, DOB, or race/ethnicity, yet every risk score and USPSTF cadence rule requires them — this is a P0-blocking type change the original draft never surfaced, and fixtures literally cannot make the engine testable until it lands.; Two of the escalation paths the draft called 'reuse' are net-new safety code: safety.ts today recognizes only blood-pressure bands and physical symptoms, so both the abnormal-lab soft-escalate (eGFR/A1c/LDL) and the depression self-harm/suicidality hard-escalate must be built and clinically reviewed — mis-scoping these as free reuse risks shipping a depression screen with no working crisis path.; The primary clinical outcome metrics (screening completion, new diagnoses, care-team rejection rate) are unmeasurable in v1 because the care-team channel is one-way with no return signal — they require a P2 clinician confirm/close surface or EHR read-back, so v1 can only report self-reported proxies.; Risk-equation population bias plus reimbursement dependency: PCE/FRAX perform unevenly across race/ethnicity (equity + false-precision risk), and whether closing screening gaps maps to funded clinic workflow (HEDIS/AWV/RPM/CoCM CPT) determines whether recommendations get actioned or become trust-eroding unactioned nudges. |
| 02 | [Triaged Between-Visit Remote Monitoring — Heart Failure & Post-Discharge](02-between-visit-monitoring.md) | FDA non-device-CDS line: rule-based thresholds that recommend a specific action, plus consuming a Bluetooth device signal (P1) and any learned threshold model, push from low-risk decision-support toward regulated SaMD — needs legal/regulatory review before P1 devices and P2 ship. The §520(o)(1)(E) exemption hinges on the clinician independently reviewing the basis, which the design supports but does not guarantee.; HIPAA/backend is a hard gate the draft under-weighted: the shipped app is local-state with no backend, so the multi-patient worklist (the flagship surface) cannot touch real PHI until a compliant backend (BAA, encryption, authz, breach logging, real audit) exists. P0 worklist is demo/fixture-only by necessity.; Audit trail cannot meet the safety/liability bar without a type change: the shipped AuditEvent has a closed action union and NO actor field, so 'who dispositioned/snoozed this and why' is unrecordable as-is. Disposition auditing is a blocking P0 dependency, not free reuse.; Reimbursement reality bites the manual-first MVP: RPM CPT (99453/99454/99457) requires ≥16 device-transmission days per 30-day period, and manual-entry days likely do NOT count — so the device-free P0 may prove the clinical thesis while being unbillable, and threshold false-positive rate (imperfect predictive value of 3lb/24h, 5lb/7d) can reintroduce alert fatigue that defeats the whole signal-not-firehose premise. |
| 03 | [Social Determinants Screening & Resource Connection (Food-is-Medicine)](03-sdoh-resource-connection.md) | Crisis-pathway gap is the sharpest safety risk: the shipped escalation offers only call_clinic/draft_message (verified CARE_TEAM_ACTIONS in safety-gate.ts:7) with NO 988/911 action anywhere in the codebase. The draft repeatedly promised a 'crisis line' that does not exist. Screening for suicidal ideation or IPV and routing to a possibly-closed clinic is clinically inadequate and creates liability - so SI/mood/IPV items are cut from P0 and gated (FR-16) behind a real crisis pathway plus clinical+legal sign-off.; SaMD / scope-of-practice edge: the feature stays non-device only while it never outputs a diagnosis or treatment recommendation. Any inference that names a condition (e.g. 'depression' from an isolation item) or triages mental health crosses the FDA line - the gated behavioral domain is the live hazard and must be held out until reviewed.; Human-in-the-loop is a hard dependency, not a nice-to-have: the CHW/coordinator is load-bearing for closing every referral loop and confirming terminal outcomes. Clinics without a named human owner cannot close referrals and should not be onboarded - otherwise the app ships stale phone-list behavior wearing a closed-loop label.; PHI egress + sensitive-data handling: per-referral consent, BAAs, and data minimization for findhelp/Unite Us and benefit agencies must clear counsel; and any future SI/SUD-adjacent capture triggers 42 CFR Part 2 segmentation that must be designed before P1, not retrofitted. Stale/wrong public-charge reassurance to immigrant patients is a real, direct harm. |
| 04 | [Integrated Behavioral Health & Crisis Escalation — PRD](04-behavioral-health.md) | FDA CDS/SaMD line: trend-based escalation rules that prompt a clinician to act can tip the app from non-device Clinical Decision Support into regulated Software-as-a-Medical-Device. The rewrite hard-codes 'surface data + route to a human, never recommend a clinical action' into FR-3 and Safety, but this must be legally confirmed BEFORE P0 ships — it is the top gating risk.; Crisis detection is incomplete and must not be marketed as complete: free-text pattern matching cannot reliably catch obfuscated, coded, or negated suicidal ideation, and PHQ-9 item-9 depends on honest self-report. Item-9 is the primary structured net and free-text is only a supplementary net; residual false-negative risk is real and must be disclosed at consent, never papered over in copy.; Persistence/HIPAA/42 CFR Part 2 gap: the app is localStorage-only today, so every clinician-facing capability (review queue, outreach, EHR write-back) is blocked until a HIPAA-compliant backend with a BAA exists — and substance-use (AUDIT-C) data triggers 42 CFR Part 2's stricter consent-to-disclose rules that most teams underestimate.; Ordering correctness is a latent safety bug if implemented naively: in the real decideSafety(), dangerous-vitals hard-escalations from stored readings run BEFORE free-text input safety (safety-gate.ts lines 48-86), so crisis_escalate must be the literal first branch of the function — 'above existing branches' is too vague and would let a co-occurring hypertensive reading demote a suicidality disclosure. This must be enforced by an explicit ordering test. |
| 05 | [Chronic-Disease Self-Management Loops + Deprescribing & Cost Transparency](05-chronic-loops.md) | FDA CDS/SaMD exposure on titration: a protocol-bounded dose-titration prompt likely fails the 21st Century Cures CDS-exemption 'independent review' prong (a patient cannot review the clinical basis), making it a regulated dosing-CDS function — the draft let it ship in P0; the final spec pulls it out and gates it behind a formal regulatory read (FR-15).; Model-generated emergency/hypoglycemia guidance is a scope-and-harm risk: 'fast-acting sugar now' is treatment advice that is dangerous if the threshold or unit (70 mg/dL vs 7.0 mmol/L) is wrong; mitigated by forced-unit entry, unit-aware deterministic thresholds, and fixed clinician-authored emergency cards instead of free-text model output.; Deprescribing/interaction ruleset is a liability and maintenance hazard: an unlicensed, stale, or non-exhaustive Beers/STOPP + drug-interaction table creates a duty-to-act problem and patient-safety exposure; requires a licensed, versioned, dated dataset with a named owner and legal/clinical sign-off before reaching patients.; Outcome metrics are un-instrumentable in the shipped app: the single-user localStorage prototype cannot measure 6-month cohort clinical outcomes or matched controls, and has no BAA-covered backend — lagging metrics are aspirational until the P2 HIPAA-eligible backend, consent, and a study design exist. |
| 06 | [Care-Coordination Quarterback — Transitions & Referral Loop-Closure](06-care-coordination.md) | Reconciliation/summary UI is the FDA SaMD boundary: any string that reads as a drug-interaction verdict or dose recommendation (vs. neutral present-and-label) reclassifies the tool as a device. FR-9 constrains it to display + provenance + neutral duplicate notices, but every reconciliation/transition string needs legal+regulatory copy review before P1.; Fidelity gap the draft masked: heart failure is NOT a modeled Condition (app ships hypertension\|diabetes\|obesity only) and there is no clinician-facing surface or backend at all. Ruth's 'CHF lens reuse,' the Coordination queue, ADT ingestion, and the actor-bearing audit trail are all net-new build, not reuse — mislabeling them under-scopes P1.; Alert fatigue is the single make-or-break failure: if triage precision drops below ~85%, clinicians abandon the queue regardless of integration quality. Batching, dismiss-with-reason, and a shadow (RN-first) rollout period must be first-class, not polish.; HIPAA + wrong-patient exposure: localStorage is a non-compliant substrate for real PHI, and low-confidence ADT matches are a combined safety+privacy hazard. P1 requires a HIPAA-grade backend, BAAs with every feed source and the LLM provider (enterprise/no-retention tier), and the quarantine-never-auto-attach matching design is load-bearing. |
| 07 | [Reaching the Unreached — Voice-First, Multilingual, Low-Literacy Access](07-access-equity.md) | Live realtime-voice bypasses the deterministic safety gate: today's WebRTC path streams model audio browser↔OpenAI and never re-enters classifySafety(), so a spoken symptom or dangerous-value utterance can get an ungated model answer. Made release-blocking as FR-6a; the original draft assumed voice parity as a property rather than building it.; Urgency conflation in the draft's own flow: SBP 182 is a HARD-escalate emergency in the real gate ('seek urgent help now'), not the 'call_clinic' soft escalate the draft showed. Softening a dangerous vital or stroke-symptom phrase into a routine clinic callback is a direct clinical-safety error; the two urgency tiers must stay distinct across every channel and language.; Regulatory exposure grows with voice + non-English + first backend: spoken threshold/urgency guidance in another language may shift the FDA SaMD / clinical-decision-support analysis; SMS is unencrypted (needs consent, content minimization, BAA); and the SMS/phone store is the app's first server-side PII store, expanding HIPAA surface beyond today's on-device model. Needs a regulatory + legal read before scaling languages/channels.; Care-team load and reimbursement are unfunded and unsafe if unstaffed: reaching more under-reached patients generates more escalations, and voice/SMS-first device-optional workflows don't cleanly satisfy RPM's device-data CPT requirements — so the added coordinator time may be unbillable, and unstaffed alerts are themselves a safety risk. Escalation volume must be modeled and staffed before scaling enrollment. |
| 08 | [Diabetes First-Class (Blood-Sugar Ownership)](08-diabetes-first-class.md) | An implementation/build spec (not an upstream PRD): makes a type-2 diabetes patient first-class via glucose logging, a condition picker, a real diabetes food lens, a Home "medicine that matters" surface, and a live grounded Coach, in six shippable phases P0–P5.; Two safety blockers caught in adversarial review and resolved inline: stored dangerous glucose readings must be wired into decideSafety (the Coach gate), and the reused numeric scanner is inert on a bare mg/dL number because hasDangerousGlucose is cue-gated — both must classify by value, or a logged severe-low never escalates.; Storage reset-to-demo trap: glucoseReadings must backfill before validation and live in the sanitize tier (never the core-reset tier), and the sanitized array must be written into sanitizedState explicitly, not only the persist-diff, or a foreign-patientId row survives.; Grounding gap: verifyGrounding has no glucose numeric verifier, so a cited-but-mismatched glucose number could pass — closed with extractGlucoseClaims + an extended prompt guard.; Condition model goes single→multi (Brent has both HTN + diabetes) via an optional conditions[] with lens composition, keeping the single-condition identity contract (selectLenses(['diabetes']) === diabetesLens) that existing callers/tests rely on. |
| 09 | [Family Navigator — Developmental Pediatrics Resource Connection (Demo)](09-family-navigator.md) | Demo-first spec for the UKHCI backlog item "provide waitlisted families with local resources and education": caregiver profile + needs screen + voice/typed interview box (LLM crunch) + verified county/age-matched Kentucky catalog + P1 diagnosis-date staged nudges.; Sharpest safety finding, execution-verified: the crisis gate is 100% first-person — "my son says he wants to die" and five sibling strings pass every safety layer unmatched today, and the 27-case corpus has zero caregiver-voice cases, so caregiver crisis rules + corpus additions gate every family free-text surface (FR-6).; Anti-hallucination is structural, not lint: the model may propose facts/domains/rationales but never resource names or diagnoses — catalog matching stays deterministic, and rationales pass the existing diagnosis-claim regexes.; Verification discipline is the product thesis, with receipts: The Arc of Kentucky is defunct yet still listed in KY FACES; IDA Kentucky's site is dead but nationally linked; helpmegrowky.com serves an expired cert — the spec ships a fetch-verified catalog AND a dated do-not-seed list.; Storage trap: the new `family` slice needs load-time backfill + guards + sanitize or existing saves hard-reset to demo; the child lives as data under the single caregiver account (hasValidRelationships forbids a second patient), and P0 deliberately adds no AiMode.; Explicitly a demo — fictional personas, demo badge, no real-family launch without proxy-consent/minors-governance work deferred to a gated P2. |
| 10 | [Screening Hub — High-Yield Questionnaire Delivery Engine (Demo)](10-screening-hub.md) | Registry-driven engine generalizing three hand-rolled questionnaires (PHQ-9, social screen, family screen) into instruments-as-data with one generic runner; ships a 2-minute front-door battery (PHQ-2/GAD-2/Hunger Vital Sign/tobacco/NIDA), KY-critical eligibility modules (lung LDCT — KY is #1 nationally in lung cancer, CRC, prediabetes), a perinatal PHQ pathway, and caregiver-completed SWYC/PSC-17/PHQ-A.; Sharpest storage landmine, execution-verified: `isAssessmentEvent` is hardcoded to `instrumentId === "phq9"` and `isValidCoreAppState` runs it per-row before sanitize, so any second instrument persisted today hard-resets the whole state to demo on reload — widening type + guard + core-validity edit + regression test must be atomic.; Crisis behavior is frozen: PHQ-9/PHQ-A item-9 positives reproduce the existing tested crisis panel byte-identically via a declarative `crisisOnPositive` rule; no new pathway, no free-text surfaces, so no new corpus rules.; Licensing is a build gate, not a style note: EPDS, M-CHAT-R/F, CRAFFT, Mini-Cog, PROMIS require written permission for app embedding and get named-but-empty slots (SWYC's POSI substitutes for M-CHAT; the perinatal pathway uses public-domain PHQ-2→PHQ-9 instead of EPDS); C-SSRS/DAST/HITS/ASQ-3/PEDS/SCARED excluded on licensing or size, and SWYC itself ships license-pending (TEAM UP Center electronic-use agreement) behind a demo-preview banner.; IPV and ACEs screening deliberately deferred until safe-UX prerequisites (privacy pre-check, quick exit, family-surface exclusion) exist as their own sprint.; Wording-integrity rule: instruments whose verbatim text is unverified ship a visible draft badge until a human checks the official PDF. |
